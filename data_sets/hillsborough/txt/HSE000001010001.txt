         Telephone call from Mr M J Jones ACC Nest Midlands 30 June 89

 Mr Jones has been asked by SgFC when the Nest Terrace can be released. He is
 aware of the filming scheduled for July llth and would like to know if a
 decision has been made on whether or not a barrier will be tested in situ and
 if so if the test will be filmed. I said we had not made a final decision on
 whether a barrier would be tested in situ but that we would prefer not to be
 filmed. I told him of our proposed meeting with Prof Smith on 3rd July and
 that we were likely to make a final decision on barrier testing on that day.

 My impression is that he would like to release the Terrace very shortly and if
 possible bring the filming date forward.

 Mr Jones will phone you on Monday 3 July.




 My own view is that no testing should be carried out at Hillsborough but that,
 if a test is still deemed necessary, a barrier of similar design to the failed
 barrier should be removed from the Terrace and tested here in the Laboratory.
 This would enable us to set up a test with sufficient instrumentation to
 measure all the relevant strains, deflections and forces. If we were to test
 in situ we would have to borrow the Eastwood Rig and, in the limited timescale
 available, could only carry out fairly basic measurements. If necessary a new
 barrier could be fabricated and tested and the results compared.

 The failure load of the collapsed barrier will never be known but we can
 deduce an approximate value. Ne know that the barrier survived a test force of
 6 kNlmetre in July 88 and Dr Eastwoods tests on barriers of similar design gave
 substantial deformations at about 7 and 9 kNlmetre. From these facts alone
 an estimate of the failure load of the collapsed barrier is about 8 kNlmetre.
 If calculations or tests gave values outside a range of 6 kNlmetre to I0
 kNlmetre I would doubt their value; however calculations and or tests on
 uncorroded barriers could be useful.

 I would have thought that SNFC are likely to remove all similar barriers in
 the Nest Terrace and replace them with ones of the latest design positioned so
’that they conform with the Green Guide. In which case SNFC are unlikely to
 object to our having one or more of these barriers to test in the laboratory.
