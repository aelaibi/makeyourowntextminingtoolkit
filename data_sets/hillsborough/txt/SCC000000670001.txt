HILLSBOROUGH TASK TEAM


PRESS STATEMENT RE SHEFFIELD WEDNESDAY F.C. V LIVERPOOL F.C.
FOOTBALL MATCH ON WEDNESDAY,         29th NOVEMBER,     1989


Social workers from Sheffield and Merseyside, together with counsellors
from voluntary organisations, will be in attendance at Hillsborough on
the evening of 29th November, when Liverpool visit Sheffield for the
first time since 15th April.    Workers will be available both inside the
ground and outside, on Leppings Lane and a local Community Centre, to
offer support to people from Liverpool and Sheffield.      The occasion will
be an emotional one for many people, and some may find it helpful to use
these counsellors.


Amongst those attending will be the four members of the Hillsborough
Task Team, a team set up by Family and Community Services Department
following the disaster.   The Task Team believe that it is important
to remember the impact that the tragedy has had on the people of
Sheffield, some of whom were amongst the spectators on the day, as well
as the rescue workers, helpers and residents who provided comfort and
assistance to the survivors.    Many Sheffield people were deeply upset by
the experience and as has been learned from disasters such as the
Bradford    fire, it can take quite some time before people realise how
much it has affected them.     Some people, for various reasons, have not
been able to talk about their feelings in order to begin to put it behind
them.


The members of the Task Team, all of whom were involved in assisting on
15th April and afterwards, are still available at Hillsborough Library
for anyone who feels they may benefit from talking about their experience,
and all contact is completely confidential.


If you think you would like to talk to someone, call on the Task Team at
the Library, or ring 734666.    This number will be open
until 10.30 p.m. on the 29th November, but is normally staffed Monday to
Friday, 9.00 a.m. - 5.00 p.m.

Amongst those attending will be the four members of the Hillsborough
Task Team, a team set up by Family & Community Services Department
following the disaster.     The Task Team believe that it is important
to remember the impact that the tragedy has had on the people of
Sheffield, some of whom were amongst the spectators on the day, as well
as the rescue workers, helpers and residents who provided comfort and
assistance to the survivors.     Many Sheffield people (even if only
remotely involved) were deeply upset by the experience and, as has been
learned from disasters such as the Bradford fire, it can take quite some
time before     people realise how much it has     affectedthem.   Seme     people,
for various     reasons, have not been able   to   talk about their •
feelings in order to begin to put it behind them.


The Task Team, all of whom were involved in assisting on 15th April
and afterwards, is still available at Hillsborough Library for anyone
who feels they may benefit from talking about their    experience, and
all contact     is completely confidential.


If you think you would like to talk to someone, call on the Task Team
at the Library, or ring 734666.     This number will be open until
10.30 p.m. on the 29th November, but is normally staffed Monday - Friday,
9.00 a.m. to 5.00 p.m.
