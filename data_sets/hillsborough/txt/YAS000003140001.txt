Date:          10 July 1989

I received a message to ring Mr Page.    I did so but he was not
there and spoke instead to Roger Morley.    He told me that there
had been a piece on the BBC News at 8.00 O'clock saying that NUPE
had issued a press statement to the effect that if there had been
ambulances at Hillsborough before the F.A. Cup semi-final had
commenced then more lives might have been saved.    In fact there
was an ambulance at the ground prior to the kick-off and another
one on standby close to the ground specifically designated to the
match. I told Roger that it may be that the piece which appeared
on the news had been lifted from the NUPE statement which we had
previously seen but I would read through that statement to check
it and would then ring Don Page later in the morning.

I rang Don Page at about 10.00 O'clock.  He no longer had a copy
of the NUPE statement and I said I would fax it through to him
but that it was unclear from the statement whether or not the
piece which had appeared on television had come from there.   We
agreed that he would make arrangements for someone to approach
the NUPE press office to get a copy of whatever it was they had
actually released.
I spoke to Don Page again later in the day. We agreed that there
was nothing we could do until they had obtained a copy of the
appropriate press release from NUPE.   At present there was some
problem with the fax machine at NUPE.    We agreed that I would
wait to hear from him.
