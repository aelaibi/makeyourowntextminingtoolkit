    Dear Mr Whalley

    I would be grateful if you could communicate greyhound racing's
    concern over certain aspects of local authority certification
    procedures relating to safety of sports grounds, to Lord Justice
    Taylor, which may be relevant to Stage II of his enquiry.

    The problems arise mainly out of the provisions in the Fire and
    Safety of Places of Sport Act 1987 which in practice do not fit
    well with racing facilities which are designed specifically for
    spectators to move freely about the stadium.

    After the Popplewell enquiry greyhound racing was assured that the
    sport would be treated differently as far as licences were
    concerned and indeed a specific voluntary Code of Practice was set
    out, known as the Green Guide, which made special provision for
    greyhound racing.

     Recently Sheffield Sports Stadium has had to accept very severe
     conditions in order to qualify for a Safety Certificate, such as
     the limitation of the number of customers into various bars and
     restaurants and compulsory presence of stewards outside the bars.
     The British Greyhound Racing Board are concerned that some of
     these stringent conditions, more appropriate to soccer stadia,
     might in the future be imposed on all greyhound tracks, both large
     and small.

     We would very much appreciate the opportunity of a short meeting
     with Lord Justice Taylor or his assessors to talk through the
     position of greyhound tracks as regards the safety of spectators.
     Any further legislation which affects sports grounds in general
     should take into account the different nature of racing crowds and
     their movements throughout a race meeting.

     Yours sincerely
