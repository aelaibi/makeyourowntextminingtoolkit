F2 CONTRIBUTION


In conducting the subsequent enquiry into the disaster, Lord
Justice Taylor asked the West Midlands Constabulary to conduct
investigations on his behalf. You suggest that West Midlands
is an inappropriate force to carry out such an investigation,
being the subject of an investigation itself.


This is not the case, however.   The investigation to which you
refer concerns the West Midlands Serious Crime Squad. In 1989
the Chief Constable of West Midlands asked an Assistant Chief
Constable of West Yorkshire to carry out an investigation into
individual complaints made against members of the Squad, and
into the Squad's work practices. The investigation by no
means covers the entire West Midlands force, and I am afraid
that it is wrong to suggest that it does.


As you know, following the completion of the Hillsborough
investigation, the Police Complaints Authority issued a press
statement announcing their decision to direct that
disciplinary charges be brought against two officers of the
South Yorkshire Constabulary. The Police Complaints Authority
are, of course, independent of the police and the Government.
