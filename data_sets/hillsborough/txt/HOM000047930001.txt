     HILLSBOROUGH STADIUM DISASTER

     Thank you for your letter of 18th July advising me of the terms of reference of your
     inquiry and requesting the assistance o f this Authority which of course will be given to
     the fullest possible extent.

    A disaster such as the one which took place at Hillsborough is normally the subject o f a
    referral to this Authority under Section 88 of the Police and Criminal Evidence Act, 1984.
    This is a voluntary referral by the police force concerned requesting independent
    supervision of the investigation of such an incident. This type of referral normally takes
    place before the receipt of any complaint. If complaints are subsequently received they
    are subsumed into the investigation. However, in this particular instance complaints were
    received from individual members of the public who were either directly involved or who
    were bereaved as a result of deaths within the stadium. Such direct complaints are
    recorded individually and normally then subject to individual investigation. However, an
    overall inquiry was set up, largely to be undertaken by the then Chief Constable of
    Cumbria Constabulary Mr L Sharpe, supervised by Captain E N Taylor, then a member
    o f this Authority.

    For ease of reference I have attached a list of the complainants, together with the number
    of individual complaints which they recorded and the reference number of the individual
    Authority file which attaches to their particular case.

    I have also enclosed photocopies of the interim statements following the completion of the
    investigation, the majority of which were issued on the 15th August 1990 or
    29th June 1990. Some few remaining investigations were concluded on other dates
    namely the 15th August and 23rd May 1990.

    These interim statements are issued pursuant to Section 89 sub section 7 of the Police and
    Criminal Evidence Act and are a statement to the effect that the Authority are satisfied
    that the matter of complaint has been investigated to the Authority’s satisfaction.

        The interim statement is issued prior to consideration of the completed investigation by
        the Crown Prosecution Service in respect of any possible criminal offences. After the
        Crown Prosecution Service have reached their decision and on the assumption that there
        are no criminal proceedings the Authority must consider any recommendations as to
        disciplinary action made by the appropriate chief officer. The Authority must determine
        whether it can agree with those disciplinary recommendations or whether it believes that
        some other action would be more appropriate.

        It may be helpful if I outline a difference in the Authority’s remit when an individual
        complaint is referred to it under Section 87(1) of the Police and Criminal Evidence Act
        as compared to a voluntary referral by the force concerned under Section 88. Had the
        matter come before the Authority as a Section 88 voluntary referral by South Yorkshire
        Police the terms of reference would, most probably, have been to investigate matters
        leading up to the crowd disruption at Hillsborough, arising during the course of that
        disruption and leading to the deaths of a number of spectators. This would have been a
        wide ranging brief which would have given the Authority the remit to investigate the
        totality of events on the day and the planning leading up to the football match itself
        However, individual complaints referred to the Authority under Section 87 are specific
        complaints against named or identifiable officers and as such the Authority’s remit is
        restricted to the matters of complaint.

       I hope you will forgive me if I draw one further matter to your attention and that is the
        question ofpublic interest immunity. As a result of the Appeal Court Judgment in Taylor
        v The Chief Constable of Greater Manchester Police (Times Law Reports 19 January
        1995), the reports of investigating officers form a class which is entitled to public interest
       immunity. I am not of course suggesting that this immunity attaches to the reports in the
       face of your inquiry. However, I would be most grateful if you would bear this point in
       mind in considering whether the conclusions and recommendations of investigating
       officers should be brought into the public domain as a result of your inquiry.

        Please be aware that the member responsible for supervision of these investigations,
        Captain E N Taylor, is now retired from the Authority as is Brigadier John Pownall who
        reviewed the disciplinary recommendations and was therefore responsible for
        recommending and ultimately directing that disciplinary charges be preferred against the
        two superintending officers at the ground. Should your inquiry wish to interview these
        retired members of the Authority I would be most grateful if the request could, in the first
        instance, be made to my office so that I can brief the members concerned on the terms
        of reference etc of your inquiry.

        Turning now to your access to our documentary evidence, first of all please be assured
        that all the files relating to these complaints have been retained within the Authority. As
        you will appreciate there is a considerable volume of evidence and other documentary
        material including tape recordings and video recordings. I would be very happy to make
        available a room within the Authority’s offices in which all the evidence could be placed
        and to which your Secretariat could be given secure access for inspection of the
        documents whenever they wish to have such access. Alternatively, I am sure that the
        Home Office could make arrangements for the documents to be collected from the
        Authority and transferred to your Secretariat should you so wish. I await your decision
        in this matter.

 HILLSBOROUGH STADIUM DISASTER



 I have been appointed by the Home Secretary to conduct a scrutiny o f possible fresh evidence
 relating to the Hillsborough Stadium disaster. I am writing to ask for your assistance (my
 Secretary Mr Bone has already spoken to you).

 It might be helpful if I explain that the objective o f my scrutiny is to establish whether there
 is any significant new evidence which was not previously available to Lord Taylor’s Inquiry,
 to the Inquests, or to the prosecuting or police disciplinary authorities. If there is significant
 evidence, it w ill be my aim to see if it would have affected the outcome o f any o f the
 proceedings. I hope to report my findings to the Home Secretary by the end o f the year. I
 attach my terms o f reference.

 In the course o f my work I expect that I w ill need to have access to the documents about the
 incident and the various proceedings arising out o f it which are held by the Police Complaints
 Authority. I should be obliged if you could arrange for the Secretariat to my scrutiny to be
 informed in writing by 4 August o f the nature o f these documents and how we may most
  conveniently obtain access to them.

  I have written to the Hillsborough Family Support Group and to various other people who
  have come forward asking for some basic summary information about the new evidence
  which is said to exist. This information w ill help me during the preliminary stages o f my
  work.

  As my scrutiny o f the evidence proceeds I may wish to interview some witnesses directly. I
  am also willing to consider interviewing individual witnesses at their request. These
  interviews w ill be private.

  The Secretariat to my scrutiny w ill be happy to discuss the arrangements if you wish. Please
note that the telephone and room numbers shown above are temporary - we w ill notify you o f
our permanent address and telephone as soon as we have them.
