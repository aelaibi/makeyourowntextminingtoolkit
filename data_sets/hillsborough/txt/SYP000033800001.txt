Rt Hon Douglas Hurd MP
Home Secretary
House of Commons
LONDON



Dear Mr Hurd


I had the misfortune to attend the football match last week where so
many people were killed and would like to bring to your attention an
important factor which, so far, has been missed by the media,
relating as to WHY the Liverpool fans were so late in arriving at the
ground compared to the fans travelling from Nottingham.

The route which I personally followed was down the Ml and we were
directed off the motorway by police signs from Junction 36. To my
amazement this converged at the same point as the traffic travelling
from Liverpool over the Pennines via the A628, A616, A629. When I
arrived at this junction, traffic was already held up (approximately
1.20pm) and this worsened as kick off time approached.

rn my opinion this is just bad police traffic control and planning.
The attached maps show clearly the stupidity of the police decision
and also a better alternative.

There is no   doubt that these delays were a contributing factor and
resulted in   the last minute panic as hundreds of fans arrived late.
The rest in   now history and I will be making these facts public
through the   media and at the public inquiry.

I trust you will appreciate the fact that I have informed you in
advance.


Your^ sincerely
