   Dear Mr Whitehurst

   Hillsborough Inquiry

   I refer to my letter of 12th May 1989 and to our discussions at
   Sheffield Town Hall on Monday 15th May 1989.

   I write regarding (i) the transcript of the hearing and                                                                                          (ii)         the
   statements taken from St John Ambulance members.

    (i)             Transcript

/ A f t e r our discussions I requested the Inquiry Secretariat to let me
   have a copy of the transcript of the hearing by first class post
   each night.     I have not yet received any transcripts.  Please would
   you let me know the position.

   {ii)             Statements of St John Ambulance Members

  As you know, we have been waiting for a considerable time to receive
  copies of the statements given by St John Ambulance members to the
  police.  Since the statements were given on a voluntary basis it is
  difficult to understand the reason for the delay and the refusal of
  the police to provide copies direct to my clients.

   I must emphasise that because our clients are not represented at the
   Inquiry it is essential that we receive copies of the St John
   Ambulance members' statements without further delay.  The contents
   of the statements might necessitate review of the question of
   whether St John Ambulance should apply for leave to be represented
   at the Inquiry.
