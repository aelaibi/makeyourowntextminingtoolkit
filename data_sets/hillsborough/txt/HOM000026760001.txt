                 Dear Sir,
                 I am in receipt of your letter of the 24th May and your enquiry into fences
                 at Portman Road, Ipswich.
                 One section of our ground in the North Stand has a perimeter fence and I
                 enclose an Architect's drawing of this area.   Sections A. and B. are designated
                 for visitors and have a fence, and section C. is for home supporters.   The
                 capacities for this area are 2,374, 1,964 and 4,652 respectively.
                 Between gates B. and C., and D. and E. there is a dividing fence which is
                 installed to the ridge of the roof. Areas shaded in green are sterile areas
                 used by the police if nec'essary. Lateral barriers are in place between the
                 first row of barriers and the perimeter wall with gaps at gates A. B. and C.
                 to allow any supporters standing immediately in front of the wall to walk to
                 the passageway to the emergency exit gates.   The size of the gates and lateral
                 walkways are as follows - Gate A. 1.14 mts. Gate B. .97 mts.         C. 1 mt.
                 D. and E. 1.2 mts.
                 Following the disaster at Sheffield on the 15th April, a meeting was held at
                 this ground on Monday 17th with all members of the Suffolk County Council and
                 Safety Committee present.    It was decided that the perimeter fencing should
                 stay at least ■until the results of your enquiry are published, as it was felt
                 that the original need for fences had not been completely removed.

                 I trust this information is satisfactory to your needs but should you have any
                 further queries please do not hesitate to get in touch.

                     Dear Sir

                     I refer to your letter dated 24 May 1989, seeking details of fencing
                     arrangements at the Elland Road Stadium.

                     May I confirm that for several years, fencing has been in position
                     around the entire pitch perimeter of the ground. Fencing to the terracing
                     areas was erected upon an order of the Football Association following
                     crowd disorders.  Fencing to the seating areas was erected following
                     the migration of a troublesome element from the terracing to seating
                     areas.

                     For the past 12-18 months, the Club have pursued                                                                            a policy, together
                     with the Local Authority, of discreetly removing                                                                            certain fences and,
                     at the present time, no fences exist to the West                                                                            Stand seating area
                     or Family Stand terrace and all spikes on top of                                                                            the majority of the
                     remaining fences, have been removed.

                     A plan of the ground is enclosed, showing (in yellow) positions of
                     pitch perimeter fencing, together with the position of exit gates etc.

                     The pitch perimeter gates, of which there are a total of 17, all measure
                     six feet in width.

                     In all areas of the ground, the pitch perimeter fence does not form part
                     of the crowd control barrier. Another load-bearing barrier is positioned
                     one metre inside the fencing which allows for lateral movement of
                     officials and police officers as well as an escape route in the case of an
                     emergency.
