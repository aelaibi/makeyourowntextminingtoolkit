Dear

Thank you for your recent letter concerning safety of sports
grounds.  I apologise for the delay in replying to you.

Lord Justice Taylor, in his Final Report into the Hillsborough
Stadium disaster, recognised that there is no single solution
to achieve crowd safety and cure all the problems of crowd
control. However, he was satisfied that the provision of
seated accommodation does more to achieve these objectives
than any other single measure. He also considered that moving
to all seated accommodation was consistent with the policies
of both FIFA and UEFA. For these reasons the government have
given their support to Lord Justice Taylor's recommendation.

Lord Justice Taylor also recognised the problems that can
arise from crowd access and perimeter fencing.  In his Final
Report he made several recommendations concerning the
admission of spectators through turnstiles and into pens. He
also recommended that the height of perimeter fencing should
be limited to 2.2 metres, all spikes or overhanging sections
should be removed from all fences and sufficient gates should
be provided to enable emergency evacuation onto the pitch.
