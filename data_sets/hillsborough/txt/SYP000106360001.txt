INSPECTOR - COMMUNITY LIAISON DEPARTMENT, WEST BAR POLICE STATION



On Saturday 15 April 1989, I was on duty at West Bar Police Station.                  I

was the Officer responsible for logistics in connection with the                    *D'

Division Operation Order for the Semi Final being held on that day at the

Hillsborough Football Ground.

Having completed my tasks during the morning oI     rhst day, - ^               "

and was sitting in the Comnunity Liaison               ac wesr              -

watching the television programme      which was reporting the activities at

the Hillsborough Football Ground pjrior^ to the Kick off of the match,

became apparant to me that there was a problem with the crowd that could

build up into a serious situation.       The staff at the Cormunity Liaison

Department at 'D' Division are responsible for manning the Police Roan at

the Hallamshire Hospital in the event of a major incident.        I consequently

informed Sergeant Thomas, Police Constabfc Mabbot, Staniforth and Atkinson

that we would be leaving immediately to staff the Casualty Bureau.                    I

informed the Control Room at West Bar of my concerns and that we were going

to the Hospital forthwith.

We   attended   at   the Hospital   taking with us the Casualty Bureau box

containing the necessary documentation.               .

On arrival at the Hospital we commenced to "seu up        Uie Bureau.   I

the senior member of medical staff in the Hospital and advised him that he

should prepare for the possibilty of a large number of casualties and he

agreed to make the necessary arrangements.

I cannot be specific about the time that the first casualty arrived at the

Hospital but we had sufficient time to organise the Bureau, fixing up the

room, telephones, documentation, etc.       There was then a steady flow of

casualties arriving at the Hospital        ranging frcrn minor to critically

injured people.       One body only came to the Hallamshire Hospital and this
M A YOUNG
was placed in the temporary mortuary.          The body could not be identified

and was eventually transferred to the temporary mortuary at Hillsborough

accompanied by a Police Sergeant.

The details of all the casualties arriving at the Hospital were obtained

by members of my staff on the attached forms.           They were then passed by

telephone to the Incident Room at Ecclesfield, this was a very laborious

and time consuming task which was only relieved when eventually, seme

considerable time later, a Fax machine ws brought to the Hospital by

Inspector Woodhead.
In addition to documenting the details of casualties and passing them to

Ecclesfield,   we    had    the   ongoing   responsibility   of   checking   on   the

condition of casualties admitted to the Hospital and establishing who had

been discharged.      There were constant enquiries frcrn relatives at the

Hospital about      "missing" people and frcrn the Social Work team at the

Hsopital who were collating lists of people who were not accounted for.

The operation at the Hallamshire Hospital ran as smoothly as could be

expected considering the number of casualties, relatives and members of

the media present.         The only real problem was one of communication with

the Ecclesfield Incident Room as many times and for long periods it was

impossible to contact them by phone.         This would have been a more serious

problem had the Fax machine not been available.
