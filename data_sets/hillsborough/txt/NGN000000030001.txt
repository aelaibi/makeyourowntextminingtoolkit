To whom it may concern,

              I am writing to say how appalled I am about the pictures
printed in your newspaper on Monday 17thApril 1 9 8 9 , concerning the Liverpool
disaster.
              Do you realise that these people you photographed being
crushed to death were somebody's son,daughter, father etc..
Do you get your kicks out of showing your readers people being crushed
to death?
              I realise you were trying to get the message across about
the dangers of football matches but did you have to print close-ups.
              Just think how the families of the dead must feel to see
someone they loved so dearly, someone they lived their life for, dying
and actually seeing the terror in thier eyes, the agony in thier faces.
Try to imagine how they must feel, how much heartbreak it brings them
to know that the last time they saw thier loved ones they were on the
front page of your newspaper dying.

letter to you. I personally did not know anyone who was there but your

    Dear Sir

    Re:    The Hillsborough Disaster/Daily Mirror/Sun


    I am writing to complain about the front and back page of the Daily
    Mirror and The Sun on 17 April 1989. They were in fact the same
    photograph but the Daily Mirror was printed in colour.

    This 'photograph is the' wirst' I have ever seen. I can see no point
    in showing this photograph which basically is of dying children.
    If there is a point I am missing here I vrould be grateful if you
    could let me know what it is.

    I think people are fully aware of the horror of that day so why
    is it necessary to go into so nuch detail. It also calls in to
    question what sort of person can stand there and take a photograph
    of dying children.

    I look forward to hearing--from you as to why the Daily Mirror and
    The Sun are allowed to print a photograph like this and what purpose
    they are serving other than to sell their sensationlist newspapers.


    Yours sincerely

Dear Sirs

re: The Sun newspaper, 17th April 1989

The Sun's coverage of the Liverpool disaster that occurred on the 15th April
was, in my opinion, very well covered in all aspects with the sadly superb photograph
in the centre pages summing up the enormity of the situation in all its horror,
fear and distress.

Unfortunately, I take great exception to the grotesque picture on the front page.
We all know what ensued that afternoon, both from television and radio and the
Sunday papers.    It is totally unnecessary for such pictorial gore to be printed for
anyone to see and it must have caused the most unimaginable distress and shock
to the families who could suddenly identify their son or daughter in such a state
of horror.   This was not realism, it was bad journalism." There is no need to
resort to the printing of this type of picture and I sincerely hope I never see
anything like it again.     This type of picture is not needed for people to under­
stand the grief and misery of what has happened,we know only too well without
it.

Yours faithfully
