MAJOR INCIDENT - HILLSBOROUGH

On Saturday 15 April 1989, I was on call Officer.      At 1532 hours, I was bleep
contacted and subsequently telephoned HQ Control at 1540 hours.   A major incident
was taking place at Hillsborough F/C and I was asked to attend.

I left home for Hillsborough requesting en-route a majax vehicle to attend, I was
informed one had been despatched.   A rendezvous point was given out on ERC as Gate
'O', where I arrived at approximately 1610 hours.   I contacted the Incident Officer
and was asked to assist with the marshalling of vehicles at the loading point.
Information that there was approximately 55 dead and that all stretcher patients
had been taken leaving a few walking   wounded to be conveyed, was passed to me.

I remained on scene until the dead     were brought to   the gym which was     used as   a
mortuary and the yard cleared of ambulances.

At approximately 1800 hours,     the Incident Officer released outside Authority
vehicles and I too was released at       this time. I would like to point out that
whilst I was there the Incident Officer organised and controlled the incident      ina
professional and competent manner.




Relief A/E Officer
