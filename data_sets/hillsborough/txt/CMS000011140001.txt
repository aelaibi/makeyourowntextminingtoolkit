D ear   Mr

Thank you for your recent letters to the Prime Minister and the
Minister for Sport.  These have been passed to this department
in view of our responsibilities for safety at sports grounds.
I apologise for the delay in replying to you.

Your view that all seated accomodation at football grounds will
not solve the problems of crowd safety and control has been
noted. However, it is a view which the government does not share
and which does not equate with the views expressed by Lord
Justice Taylor in his Final report on the Hillsborough Stadium
disaster.   He recognised that there was no single solution to
achieve total safety and cure all the problems of crowd control.
However, he was satisfied that seating does more to achieve these
objectives than any other single measure.   Further more this is
in line with the policies of both FIFA and UEFA.

I have also noted your concern that seating will be installed
onto existing terrace slopes and that the view from such seats
will be poor. The Guide to Safety at Sports Grounds ("The Green
Guide") states that:
                       "...the calculation of the capacity of the
standing area should take into account only those parts of the
viewing accomodation from which the whole of the playing area can
be seen..."

Similarly we would expect this criteria to be met in seated areas
aswell.  We expect the guidelines set out in the Green Guide to
be followed by local authorities when determining the conditions
of a ground's safety certificate.

Once again I apologise for the delay in replying to you.

                                                        Yours sincerely
