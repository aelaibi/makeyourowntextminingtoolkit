BUILDING CONTROL PROFESSIONAL A

       We recently held one of our regular liaison meetings
with the five ( ! ) professional associations who represent
building control officers.  I noted a number of points which
may be of wider interest -

         i.       The Institute of Building Control are (at my
         original    suggestion)  developing    links   with   their
         opposite numbers in other EC member states.          I have
         asked for a list of their contacts, which may be
         useful.   The Commission have apparently suggested that
         they should set up a European organisation - which the
         Commission will     then consult    on   Building   Control
         matters.

         ii.     The London District Surveyors Association have
         completed work on a Code for Atria Buildings.   I have
         been promised a (free!) copy.

         iii.    LDSA have also set up a working party on
         structural problems.  Mr Kalra may wish to find out
         more.

         iv.     IBC hope to have a number of papers on Safety
         at Sports Grounds at their Annual Conference next
         month. (copy of programme attached).        They had a
         working party on safety at sports grounds a year or two
         ago - I will get details if Mr Kinghan is interested.

         v.      Although we left this until next time, there
         seemed to be some feeling that it is no longer
         necessary to control cavity fill.

         vi.     Finally,  a   point  for   our own review  of
         administrative aspects of the Regulations. Do we need
         to give further thought to who is responsible for
         payment of a building control fee?
