            DRAFT SUBMISSION TO MINISTERS: REVIEW OF ALL-SEATING POLICY

            You sought comments on your draft submission to Ministers
            recommending a review of the all-seating requirement at football
            grounds. These are as follows:-

            Paragraph 3
            The final sentence is inaccurate - the Home Secretary accepted
            the Taylor recommendations in principle, but chose to limit the
            requirement for all-seating to Football League clubs, rather than
            extending it to all sports grounds designated under the 1975 Act,
            as Taylor recommended. We would prefer

                    'The Home Secretary accepted these recommendations in
                    principle, and accepted the Taylor timetable for their
                    implementation at Football League grounds.    However,
                    following    consultation   with   the    police   and
                    representatives of other sports, it was decided to
                    confine the all-seated requirement only to the grounds
                    of Football League clubs.

            Paragraph 4
            I do not think we should suggest that the Minister 'may wish to
            consider reverting to a position similar to that preceding
            publication of the Taylor Report'. This would seem to imply that
            Taylor, and therefore the Government,got it wrong. Would it not
            be better presentationally to say

                    '...you may wish to consider reviewing the all-seater
                    policy to ensure that it targets those clubs where the
                    risk is greatest more effectively than the present
                    blanket approach which, in the light of experience,
                    now smacks of overkill.'




S 363
•   •
        Paragraph 5

        Fourth line - delete 'on the grounds of' and replace with 'by
        reference to' . (Forgive the nitpicking, but given the subject
        matter the word 'grounds' has various meanings.)

        You mention crowd control and the risk of crowd "surging" as
        being the primary reasons for the introduction of all-seating -
        but we have never seen any backing papers which indicate that
        this was a main factor and it is the first time we have seen it
        advanced as a 'primary reason'.

        Paragraph 6 (i)

        Seventh line   - should read   'average attendances   of   7,000 or
        fewer'

        Paragraph 6 (ii)

        If the review is to include 2nd Division clubs, there will of
        necessity need to be an extension of the deadline.     The 5 or 6
        weeks between the announcement of the review and its outcome will
        almost certainly see 2nd Division clubs postponing redevelopment
        work until they know which way the wind is blowing.

        Time lost during the close season will be all but impossible to
        make up before the start of the new season, and if the decision
        goes against 2nd Division clubs, they will argue that they have
        been doubly penalised - the review caused them to delay, postpone
        or cancel their plans, and the FLA will still be insisting that
        the number of spectators admitted to their grounds is reduced
        until seats have been installed.

        Paragraph 6 (iii)

        Fourth line - delete 'can be made' and insert 'is'. At present
        the paragraph reads as if all terracing is by its very nature
        unsafe.  To accept that premise undermines the argument for a
        policy review.

        Paragraph 6 (iv)

        Is this paragraph strictly relevant to the submission? Surely
        such matters will fall into place once the reviewers have
        reported (eg if the suggestion at 6(iii) is followed, numbers
        will already be limited to safe levels).

        If we are to include this paragraph, should we also include
        something about transitional arrangements for clubs which have
        been promoted?

        Paragraph 6 (v)

        We would question the wisdom of tinkering with the Green Guide
        and advise against it, particularly in the timescale envisaged.
        The problem is one of policy change and enforcement, rather than
        of the quality of available guidance (which in any case we
        consider satisfactory).
■   »
    Paragraph 8

    In line 8 insert "certification" after 'safety1, and in line 10
    correct the spelling of 'ensure'.

    Paragraph 10

    As it stands, the first sentence reads as if the Membership
    Scheme were still under consideration. We would suggest

         'The Football Spectators Act 1989 is not now being
         used for the purpose for which it was originally
         intended.   The main thrust of the licensing regime
         post Taylor is to effect the conversion to all­
         seating, but the relevant provisions of the Football
         Spectators Act 1989 were framed with the intention of
         supporting the national membership scheme, which was
         subsequently shelved.'

    If you retain mention of Treasury's insistence on full cost
    recovery you should be aware that it has not been finally agreed
    what full cost recovery should be aimed for; that of the
    licensing function of the FLA as favoured by us or of the whole
    cost of the FLA as favoured by HMT.   This battle was postponed
    but it is one I fancy the Treasury will win.

    The paragraph goes on to suggest that consideration might be
    given to amending the Safety of Sports Grounds Act 1975 rather
    than using the licensing system as the means by which to achieve
    all-seating.

    Unfortunately, we have been unable to unearth very much to
    explain why this option was originally rejected in favour of a
    late amendment the Football Spectators Bill. Although I would
    not argue against exploring the possibilities, I would suggest
    that the basis of the 1975 Act is to ensure 'reasonable safety',
    and that since the requirement to install seats goes beyond that
    aim (standing not being intrinsically unsafe) it may be difficult
    to frame suitable amendments without doing violence to spirit of
    the original.    Also, if we are not to extend the all-seater
    policy to other sports,       the amended Act will have        to
    differentiate between football- and other sports grounds (another
    potential can of worms).     All this, I am glad to say, is a
    problem for the lawyers but is unlikely to be easily resolved.

    Paragraph 11

    I would reiterate the point that the 2nd Division clubs will need
    an extension to the deadlines as a prerequisite of inclusion in
    the review.

    I hope this is helpful. Please get in touch with David Webb,
    Steve Bilbe or me if you wish to discuss any of the points
    further.
