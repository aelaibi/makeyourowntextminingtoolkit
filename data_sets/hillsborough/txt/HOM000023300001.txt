Dear Bob,

              re:- Incident at Headinqley: 5th November. 1989.

Further to our telephone conversation regarding the incident which occurred
during the Yorkshire Cup Final at Headingley on 5th November, 1989, please
find enclosed the Police Report, the referee's official report, and a letter
from a spectator which is typical of a much greater volume of correspondence
received directly by Featherstone Rovers RLFC.

The Streethouse gang referred to in Chief Superintendent Stonely's report
have been active in West Yorkshire over the past 18 months and have caused
problems at a number of events not exclusively sporting.     It seems on 5th
November, 1989, they selected the Yorkshire Cup Final as their target.

I have to say that a considerable body of evidence has it that the crowd
surge was caused largely by some nine policemen who had stationed themselves
at; the top of the terracing in order to keep a vigilant eye on the gang.
Following some catalytic action - probably the throwing in the air of beer -
these policemen dashed forward down the terracing to confront those involved
in creating the disorder.    It seems certain that it was this action by the
police which caused the surge which resulted in innocent bystanders seeking
refuge on the running track surrounding the playing area.     To the best of
my knowledge and contrary to initial reports, no injuries where sustained as
a result of the surge.    There also appears to be no direct evidence of the
coin throwing referred to in the police report.   Certainly, there are those
who claim that the police action was extremely heavy handed and
indiscriminate.   It has caused strong feeling in the Featherstone area and
we probably have not heard the last of the business in this particular
regard.    However, the Streethouse gang are a menace and the police acted
with alacrity and decision to eject those they believed were the instigators
of the disorder.       No further incidents were reported on what was
essentially an extremely enjoyable day and I am convinced that this
particular incident, although deplorable, was an isolated event attributable
to a particular group well known to the police.
