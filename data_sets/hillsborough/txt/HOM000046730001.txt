We have now had a chance to look at the material which you enclosed with your letter o f 21
August.

As Lord Justice Stuart-Smith explained when he wrote to you in July, his task is to establish
whether there is any significant new evidence about the disaster at Hillsborough football
Stadium which was not available to Lord Taylor’s Inquiry, to the Inquests, or to the
prosecuting and police disciplinary authorities. The material you have sent us outlines
difficulties you have had with the Hillsborough Steering Group and over legal representation
at Gary’s Inquest.

We entirely agree that these difficulties are important, and we were interested to see the
papers you sent us. But they are not as we see it evidence about the disaster or things into
which Lord Justice Stuart-Smith can enquire as part o f his scrutiny. You may be interested to
see the Judge’s precise terms o f reference, and I attach these.

Your earlier letter to the Judge, dated 23 July, said that you had possible new evidence about
the disaster. We are still o f course very interested to hear o f any new evidence you may
have and it would be a great help to Lord Justice Stuart-Smith in his work if you would let us
know, however briefly, what that evidence is.

Please write or telephone if you wish.
