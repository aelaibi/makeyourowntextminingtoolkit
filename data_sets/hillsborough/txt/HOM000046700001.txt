 Thank you for your letter of 14 August to Lord Justice Stuart-Smith.


 We appreciate your having written. You say in your letter that there is vital evidence about
 Paul which should have been put before the jury at the Coroner’s inquest but was not. I am
 sure it would help Lord Justice Stuart-Smith in his work if you would let us know, however
 briefly, what that evidence is. That would allow him to make his own assessment of the
 problem. As the Judge said when he m ote to you last ; ;onth, he is happy to receive
 information from family members direct, although you in ay of course prefer to let us know
 what you want to say through the Family Support Group.


 Please write or telephone if you wish. We are quite happy to ring back if people are
 concerned about the cost of telephoni ng London from Merseyside.
