    Further to our letter dated 17 November 1989 enclosing the
    technical file for the CLOEZ ANTIPANIC BARRIER system, and
    with reference to M r . Cloez's discussions in Nimes with
    Professor Maunder, we have pleasure in submitting herewith
    solutions to the problem of the potentially dangerous
    spectator 1sump 1 traps existing at many British football
    stadia.  These would permit the installation of the CLOEZ
    system.

    Please find attached sketches to clarify our explanations           :

    ANNEXE I - EXISTING DESIGN

    IIlustrates the current stadium design as described to us.

    ANNEXE II - SOLUTION N° 1, MODIFICATION OF SPECTATOR AREA

     * Figure 1 illustrates the most economical solution, where
       the angle of the terraces is modified, without changing
       the spectator capacity.
     . Figure 2a includes a lateral spectator circulation area
       adjacent to the CLOEZ barriers and the possibility of
       extending the spectator area at the rear of the terraces
       (where space permits).
     . Figure 2b shows the modification of the terrace angle and
       rear extension of the spectator,area.
     * Figure 3 illustrates how the capacity of the stands can
       be increased by modifying the angle and installing the
       CLOEZ barriers further out into the central play area
       (feasible where pitch lines are sufficiently distant).

    ANNEXE III - SOLUTION N° 2, MODIFICATION OF PITCH LEVEL

    This solution is radical and would apply to the totality of
    the spectator viewing area around the pitch.   It would only
    be feasible when the underground water table is inferior to
    the lower level of the stands and terraces. However, since
    no modification of the viewing areas would be necessary, it
    could merit financial consideration.
                                                                 2




    Whichever of the above configurations is preferred, the
    inferior part of the viewing areas would be on a level
    with the pitch and the installation of CLOEZ ANTIPANIC
    BARRIERS would permit the rapid evacuation of part or all
    of the terraces in cases of uncontrolled crowd movement,
    whatever the cause.

    To realise the modification of the angle as described in
    SOLUTION N° 1 (ANNEXE I) as well as the reorganisation of
    spectator capacity, the system used must be, on the one hand,
    lightweight enough to avoid excessive and-irregular pressure
    on the existing structure, and thus costly reinforcing of the
    foundations, and on the other hand, robust enough to support
    the weight of the spectators and to resist all shock or

•   interference.

    Such a system already exists, and has been installed in
    stadia in France to increase crowd capacity.

    It consists of a tubular frame used principally in the
    public works and construction industry to support very
    heavy concrete beams and blocks.  On to this frame are
    fixed prefabricated, tight-fitting elements in 'fibreglass
    concrete' (which are incidentally manufactured under
    licence in the U.K.).  Among the advantages of this system
    is its resistance to all types of impact, to adverse
    weather conditions and, above all, to fire.  In addition,
    it can be installed without the necessity to modify
    existing structures and can be dismantled if desired for
    use elsewhere.

    We have pleasure in enclosing a photograph showing the
    installation of this system on site, together with some
    technical documentation and a drawing (ANNEXE IV) of the
    principle of stadium extension.

    We should be pleased to give you any further information
    you may require on this additional solution to stadium
    safety problems and to arrange a visit to a stadium in
    France which has installed the system.

    We look forward to hearing from you.
