I am writing further to your interview with Lord Justice Stuart-Smith here on Tuesday 18
November.

During the interview you told the Judge about the statement which you made to Sergeant
Appleton o f the West Midlands police at your home. You told him that Ms Appleton wrote
out the statement and that, although you signed it at the bottom, you did not read or sign the
endorsement at the top of the statement.

Since your interview with the Judge, we have obtained the original of the statement which
you made on that occasion. I attach a copy o f it. You will see that it bears what purports to
be your signature at the top of the first page, under the endorsement. You told the Judge in
your interview with him that, if the statement were signed at the top in that way, it would be a
forged signature (page 16 o f the transcript o f your interview, lines 18 to 20).

The signature which appears under the endorsement does however look similar to your
signature at the bottom o f each of the five pages, which you say you did put there (page 16,
lines 34 and 35).

We would be grateful for any comments you may have on this.
