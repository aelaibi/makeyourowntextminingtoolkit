         Dear S i r ,

         The Hillsborough Disaster


          I must protest at the waste of time and money in appointing no
          less than two public enquiries - and no end of private ones -
          into this matter.

         We all know that this tragedy could have been averted by the
         simple expedient of manning the gates with journalists of the
         popular press, and television presenters, instead of policemen.

          I look forward to these people volunteering to supervise crowds
          of well-behaved, utterly angelic, football-fans in future.  It is
          obviously so easy that I can't really believe that anything
          happened.


          Yours faithfully,
          VINCENT HALE




          The Editor,
          The Star,
          York Street,

Dear Mr. Marshall,
                   Pleke try not to feel any anguish
over thedecision    which you took at Hillsborough.
                   The press and the TV pundits cause
more upset andtrouble than anything else in present
day society. Most of us find it stalling the way they
seek to blame someone the very second that they hear
of an accident of any sort, and always giving the
impression that they themselves are whiter than white;
most of us of course hold them in very low regard, and
are sickened- at times by their lack of good taste.
                   There is one thing of which you can
be sure - if you had not opened the gate, these same
people would still have condemned you. The people with
whom I have discussed this tragedy all agree on this
point. They also agree that the blame rests with the
louts and yobs who class themselves as "football fans"
and the players, managers, and clubs who covertly
encourage them in their unacceptable behaviour
                   You were no more to blame than I,
and I was abroad at the time. Keep your chin up, the
real people of this world support you.

         Dear Roger

         Words cannot possibly express my sorrow for all concerned with
         Saturday's tragedy.

         In particular my heart goes out to you personally for the stress
         which you are currently having to face along with your colleagues
         in the force. This dreadful accident affects us all within the
         game and we cam only pray that a similar occurence is never
         experienced again in our lifetimes.

            Unfortunately, as we all know, but for the mindless few there would
            be no need for a strong police presence at any match and those who
            do attend matches with acts of violence included in their motive
            should consider themselves responsible for the deaths of many.

            Should there be any way in which I can personally be of assistance to
            you over the next few months, please do not hesitate to contact me.

            Fight the good fight!          Kindest regards.

            Yours sincerely
