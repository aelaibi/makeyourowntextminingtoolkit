JOHN BOTFIELD

39 yrs B

Police Constable 2585

Traffic Ops North, Carcroft Ind Est, Carcroft



I    am    a    Police    Constable    in    the    Road    Traffic   Division    of    the     South

Yorkshire Police stationed at Carcroft.

On Sunday 15th April 1989 I was detailed to work 8am x 8pm in company with

PC 1263 Shephard and our duties were to provide extra motorway cover on

the Ml between Tinsley and Haig in respect of football semi final traffic.

We    commenced our patrol of the Ml at 10am and nothing untoward occured

until 12.25 pm on the southbound carriageway at Tankersley when we stopped

at a transit van parked on the hard shoulder with 2 youths urinating at

the side of the motorway. PC Shephard dealt with the driver and I dealt

with the 2 youths who were reported for urinating in a public place.                              The

van       was   checked    for   alcohol     but    none   was   found.   What   was    found were

several plastic bags full of empty beer cans and all the youths in the van

approximately 8-10 with the exception of the driver had been drinking but

at that time were not drunk.

We resumed patrol which was*normal until 3.06pm when we were directed to

Gate      'O’ at Hillsborough Stadium due to some sort of major incident.                         Due

to our distance and the amount of traffic I estimate it took us some 10-15

minutes to arrive.

On     arrival     we     went   to   Gate    'O'   where    large    numbers    of    people    were

gathered,        a vast majority of which were incensed and abusive and a good

number were drunk.

On    entering      the   stadium    I    saw    a       large       number   of   people    gathered      and

persons lying injured or dead on the ground with people wandering around

aimlessly.

I gave assistance to several persons who were injured and also assisted

carrying people on makeshift stretchers who appeared to be dead.

Throughout       this     time    there   was        a       general air of hostility towards              the

police and, I formed the impression, to anyone in uniform.

There were numerous persons shouting at us while we were assisting and a

vast number of these had been drinking, they were abusive and one man in

particular was bordering on violence.

Ambulances began to arrive but due to the utter confusion were not getting

to the scene quick enough.

Together with other Road Traffic Officers we set up a corridor which we

kept    clear and organised a system to get the ambulancemen through with

minimum delay.

I remained in this duty until released from the scene.

I can say from my experience and knowledge that on our arrival confusion

reigned.       It was a difficult situation but within a very short period of

time police officers were organised                          and efficiency took over        with officers

being given specific tasks.               *

The vast majority of persons outside the ground were the worse for drink,

they    were     abusive     to    the    police             and I   saw   on a    number    of    occasions

officers being spat on although unable to do anything about it.

The    mood    of   the    crowd    was       ugly       and    I    sensed   that   not    much   would    be

required to start a riot.
