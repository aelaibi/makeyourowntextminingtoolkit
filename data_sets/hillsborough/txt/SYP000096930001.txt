Dear Mr.Wright,

I am advised by Sir Cecil Clothier that the correct way to register
a complaint about misconduct is to lodge such a complaint with yourselfg
in the first instance.

In the immediate aftermath of the Hillsborough Disaster, but after the
Home Secretary had announced the establishment of the Taylor In q u i r y , and
after the solicitor acting for a number of the families of victims had
instituted proceedings against the South Yorkshire Police, a number of
p r e j u d i c i a l comments were made about supporters of Liverpool Football Club.
These remarks were attributed to members of the South Yorkshire Force and
constitute a breach of professional standards in addition to being deliberately
designed to damage the reputation of the Club's supporters.

My complaint is that these remarks should never have been made. If there is
substance to them they should be repeated at the Inquiry and the identity of
those who made these comments made clear. I, therefore, hope that you will
investigate this matter and bring the necessary disciplinary proceedings.

Dear Mr

Thank you for your letter concerning the events at Hillsborough on 15 April.

I appreciate and understand the concerns you express in the letter but regret
that, in view of the current judicial enquiry into the tragedy, I am unable
to comment upon the policing arrangements for the match. May I express my
deepest sympathy to yourself and all those present.

The incident involving the coloured officer is another matter and one which
can be investigated immediately. You say that you do not wish to prosecute
this man but do not indicate whether you want to make an official complaint.
I enclose for your information a leaflet about complaints against the police
which may assist you in deciding whether the actions of that individual
officer should be investigated.

A stamped addressed envelope is enclosed for your reply.

Yours sincerely




Assistant Chief Constable
(Discipline & Complaints)
