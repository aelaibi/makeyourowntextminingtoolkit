REPORT OF BELGIAN COMMISSION ON SAFETY AT SPO RTIN G EVENTS


1. The Belgian Press reports that L o u i s .Tobback (Minister of the
Interior) has just received the conclusions of the Commission's
enquiry into the state of safety at first and second division
football stadiums.   The Commission reports that safety at many
stadiums (the Commission has visited most of them since it started
work in 1985) has improved but is not yet entirely satisfactory.
In a number of cases (e.g. at Anderlecht, Brugge, Cortrai and
St. Trond) only minor measures remain to be taken; what is lacking
is not money to accomplish these measures but the willpower to do
so.  In other cases (stadiums of F.C. Antwerp and RFC Liege are
particularly singled out for attention) safety measures are totally
inadequate and a great deal remains to be done : replacement of
wooden seating, renewal of electric wiring and gas installations,
putting safety exits in working condition are all mentioned.

2.  The Commission identifies the reasons for the lack of adequate
action to implement safety standards as two-fold : proprietors of
first division stadiums appear to regard themselves as powerful
and important enough to pay mere lip-service to the Commission's
requirements, making promises which they do not intend to carry
out; in the second division the crowds are smaller, organised,
hooliganism is rare and the risks therefore perceived as quite low.
Minister Tobback has now written to the owners of all stadia
pointing out exactly what is wrong in each case and exactly what
needs to be done.  He has also written to town Mayors, who have the
responsibility for ensuring that safety standards are met.   He asks
for a response in every case by 15th of August.  To strengthen his
hand, the Minister has sent to all concerned copies of a Royal Decree
published on 20th July which lays down standards of safety (crowd
capacity, construction materials and standards, safety equipment,
emergency exits, training of staff etc) which every stadium must
adopt within a set period if it is to be permitted to continue to
host large crowds for sporting events.
