HILLSBOROUGH: COST OF INQUIRIES BY WEST MIDLANDS POLICE


Lord Ferrers met a deputation from the West Midlands Police
Authority this morning to discuss the costs arising from the West
Midlands Police investigation into the Hillsborough disaster.

2.   During this meeting West Midlands representatives claimed
that because their inquiries were not mutual aid (or possibly
even if they had been mutual aid) they had no power to meet the
accommodation costs of the large number of their officers (some
350) who were involved.   They said that it would be ultra vires
to provide for accommodation outside their own police area.    It
was agreed at the meeting to seek legal advice on this particular
point and I should therefore be grateful for your views.

3.   Under the mutual aid circular payments for lodgings and
subsistence^ as applicable are reimbursed by the aided authority
to the aiding authority, where provision has not been made in
kind by the aided authority.

4.   It would( therefore appear that there is no doubt that the
aiding authority can pay the initial accommodation costs for any
officers whom they make available and then seek reimbursement.
The statutory provision for mutual aid is fairly broad since it
refers to "constables or other assistance". Although this is not
technically mutual aid, since an outside force was called in
because the need for an independent investigation was felt to the
paramount, we believe it could be classed as analogous to mutual
aid.

5.   In the current circumstances I would have thought that there
is no reason why West Midlands could not cover the costs of their
mens  lodgings  in South Yorkshire and Merseyside and seek
reimbursement from the South Yorkshire Police Authority, along
with the other additional costs which they are incurring.  Indeed
we suspect that they were bringing this up as something of a
debating point, but as they claimed to have a genuine concern we
thought it best to offer to look at the point.
6.   Lord   Ferrers   would   like   to   give   West    Midlands     an   early
reply.
