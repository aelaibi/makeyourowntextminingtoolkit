             Dear Mrs Sefton

             I refer to your letter of 6 September 1989 which has been received at my
             office.  It is the first letter I have received and I can find no trace
             of your earlier communications.

             It was most pleasing to read your very complimentary comments about the
             way in which members of West Midlands Police have been conducting
             themselves in connection with their enquiries.

             I have made a note of your very complimentary remarks in respect of
             Inspectors Layton and Sawers, Sergeants Appleton and McCombie and their
             colleagues.  I have passed on your note of appreciation for their
             information, together with my personal congratulations.

             I am obviously pleased that you are finding help through our efforts and
             obviously others. May I wish you that this continues. My continuing
             sympathies go out to you at this time.

             Yours sincerely
                                                         D/Chief Supt Foster

                                                         Please pass on my congratulations to the
                                                         officers concerned.
