                               WILL STATE:-


My    date     of       birth is 26th May, 1969-           I am employed        in     the
Engineering— Industry                  as   a   Surface Grinder    and    I     have     a
part-time job as a Turnstile Operator with Sheffield Wednesday
Football       Club.           The 1988/89 Season is the 4th seasorr-when                I
have done that work.


On     15th    April, 1989 I was instructed to man Turnstile F,                         at
the    Leppings Lane end of the Ground.                    I recall that I        opened
the     turnstile for business sometime around 12.15 - 12.30 p.m.
having        earlier          signed on for work at about 11 a.m.              When     I
signed        on    I noticed that at the Penistone Road area                    of    the
Ground        Nottingham          Forest fans were already queuing              up,    but
certainly          when        I opened the Leppings Lane turnstile we                were
initially          not    busy         at all up to about 2 p.m.         People       were
coining through slow - nothing like the sort of crowd one would
expect        for an F.A. Cup semi-final.              However after 2 p.m.            the
crowd     started          to     build up and gradually it felt           as     though
there     was       a     huge     crush of people outside        pushing        to    get
through.


To the best of my recollection, from about 2.40 p.m. or there­
abouts,        some       of     the     fans trying to     get   in   were      showing
evidence of distress.                   People were screaming at us to open the
gates     to ease the crush and I recall that at one stage one of
the     fans       who -v got,*through.,told. ,ae. as he :came' - through             •that •
3 people were dead outside the ground.                      I did not know whether
that     was right or not but certainly I knew that the situation
was frightening with the crush of people coming through, and I
was     taking a lot of verbal abuse from the Liverpool fans.                               I
found     the situation very frightening - I was working behind a
wire mesh grill and I certainly had the impression that if the
wire     mesh          grill       was not there to protect me          I   would        have
suffered          injury          at the hands of some of those fans. Many                 of
them appeared to                  be the worse for drink.


I     recall that a number of people coming through had the wrong
tickets          -     they       had coloured tickets for       Nottingham          Forest
supporters             but the Police instructed me to let them                   through.
When     the          crush developed a number of people also climbed                      in
over the top of my turnstile and some drunken fans appeared to
be     lifted          or assisted over the turnstile by               their      friends.
The position generally seemed capable of getting out of hand.


At     one       stage out of the corner of my eye I saw a                     number      of
people       go in through a large gate at the side of the                          Ground.
To the best of my recollection the Police then sought to close
the     gate on the fans coming through and they seemed to get it
about        half          closed       and I had the impression       that       the '.gate
itself       might have been buckled or broken.                      Then however         they
managed to close it fully.


Later        I       knew        that the crush had stopped, this           was     perhaps
about        3.10 p.m. and as soon as people stopped coming                         through
we     closed          down’the turnstiles and locked the                doors.          After
that     we          were        able    to   look   through   the    windows       of     the
turnstiles                 and    I recall people being carried out            apparently
injured           and ambulances and fire engines arrived.                    There       were
Liverpool              fans       who were giving us a good deal of            abuse       and
behaving              in     a threatening manner, and I recall that some                   of
them     kicked the door open but it was quickly closed again.                               A
window in one turnstile was also ■broken:and .there,,;.seemed; to .be,,
dozens           of        Liverpool      fans, about to converge ...upon         u,s.     The
Turnstile Operators decided therefore that as a group we would
 all exit together to go to the Ticket Office where we make our
                                     -   3   -




return, and we left walking out into Leppings Lane where there
were about 12 Mounted Police but no fans.


As    we   walked around the corner towards the Ticket Office           we
saw the ambulances there and there were people laid out having
mouth      to   mouth    resuscitation and I saw 2   people    who     were
apparently dead.


Having       checked    in   at the Office I then went      back   via the
Leppings Lane end to my car and drove home.


I    would      say that on the day of this semi-final I       found    the
whole      situation     with the Liverpool fans a   very     frightening
one.       Many of them coming through without tickets offered           me
money      to let them through, but I accepted money from none           of
them.         I saw no evidence of forged tickets being used but one
fan     did     get    through handing me an old   Sheffield    Wednesday
ticket and was gone before I realised what had happened.


I     have read this Statement and it is true and        correct to the
best of my knowledge and belief.
