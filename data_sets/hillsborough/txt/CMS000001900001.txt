  The purpose of this minute is to notify you formally of your
  budget for 1989/90, to outline the conditions which attach to the
^^operation of the budget, and to advise you of the extent of the
^^authorities delegated to you in respect of manpower and virement.

  2. Details of your budget are given in USBUD6 3 a copy of which is
  attached as Annex A. The total amount of the budget net of
  budgeted receipts (see pages 17 - 18 of the Guide for budget
  holders) may not be exceeded without my prior approval. Wherever
  practicable, decisions to vary the total amount of the budget in­
  year will be made only after consideration by the Budget
  Management Group (BMG), comprising myself as chairman, you and the
  other sub-budget holders. The BMG will meet at least quarterly
  and, inter alia, will continue to review significant changes made
  or proposed to the deployment of staff and other resources within
  budgets. Mr Clayton, as Secretary to the BMG, should be kept
  informed of changes which you make.

  3. In operating the budget you must at all times comply with the
  requirements of "Government Accounting" and other financial and
  accounting rules laid down by the Central Departments. Guidance on
  these and other matters is given in the section on ground rules in
  the Guide for budget holders. In cases of doubt you should consult
  the appropriate part of the Finance and Manpower Department or the
  Establishment department.
Delegated Authorities

4. The extent of your authorities in respect of manpower and
virement is set out below.

     Manpower

     4.1. Subject to keeping within the running costs cash limit
     of the budget, you are authorised to:

          a. create or abolish posts and/or change the grade mix
          in your command for all grades up to and including Grade
          7 (and equivalent);

          b. approve the recruitment of casual seasonal staff and
          of non-mobile grades to the extent delegated by, and in
          accordance with conditions and procedures agreed with
          the establishment Department;

          c. recruit sandwich students through Establishment
          Department; and,

          d. approve the working of overtime.

    4.2. In exercising your authority under (a) - (c) above, you
    should continue to observe existing grading criteria seeking
    advice from M Division as necessary. You should also ensure:
    that any changes to the deployment of staff within your
    command are cleared in advance with the appropriate personnel
    division; and that the Trade Union side is informed/consulted
    in accordance with the departmental agreement on
    "complementing, grading and organisation changes", the key
    points of which are summarised on pages 26 - 27 of the Guide
    for budget holders.
    Virement

    4.3. You will be able to switch money between component sub
         heads in the same sub-head except where this would
         involve virement of non-running costs provision into
         running costs. Virement into running costs requires
         prior agreement by Finance Division 1 and will not
         normally be possible. The reverse - virement into non'
         running costs - is permissible but Finance Division 1
         should always be notified in advance.

5. Procurement authorities will remain unchanged and subject to
conditions prescribed by the Home Office Procurement Unit (HOPU).

6. Copies of this minute go to the Budget Support Group (M
Division), Finance Division 1, Accounts Branch, Internal Audit and
the National Audit Office. A copy also goes, for information, to
the Secretary to the Home Office Trade Union Side.
