                     Mr Whitehurst

     Hillsborough Inquiry

     I refer to our telephone conversations this week and write to
     confirm our position.

     We do not at present know of any facts as to necessitate an
     application by St John Ambulance Brigade for leave to be represented
     at the Inquiry hearing.  We will be attending the first day of the
     hearing as observers but have advised our clients that further
     attendance should not be necessary, provided that:-

                      (1)                we are able to review transcripts of the proceedings on
                                         each day of the hearing; and

                      (2)                we will be notified if any evidence to be given to the
                                         hearing refers to St John Ambulance Brigade.

    We are, of course, extremely concerned to ensure that,
    notwithstanding our absence from the hearing, we will be in a
    position to prepare and make any necessary application to the
    Inquiry if any matters concerning our clients should emerge.  We
    should therefore be grateful if you would confirm that:-

                      (i)                a copy of the transcript will be available to us at the
                                         end of each day (we could make arrangements to have it
                                         collected); and

                      (ii)               we will be given advance notice, and a copy of the
                                         statement of the witness, if a witness is to be called
                                         who is likely to give evidence regarding St John
                                         Ambulance Brigade.
