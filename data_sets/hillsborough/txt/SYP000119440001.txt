IMMEDIATE ACTION AND DUTIES


1     INITIAL ACTION AND DUTIES

1.1   Protection of Body and Preservation of the Scene

      Cordon off area and protect with men, ropes, stakes.
      Consider use of tarpaulin or scenes of crime tent in inclement weather and
             portable generator. (See Part R , Chapter 7)
      Consider setting up road blocks.
      Ensure detention of suspects and keep witnesses at scene.

      Assistant Chief Constable (Operations).
      Detective Chief Superintendent/Deputy. (Appendix “H ”)
      Divisional Chief Superintendent. (Appendix “E ”)

              Detective Chief Inspector. (Appendix “G”)

      Pathologist
      Scenes of Crime Officer. (Appendix “J ”)
      Consider police dogs and horses (where required)

      Experience has also shown that it is advantageous to call in the Forensic
      Laboratory expert at an early stage —consider this option.

1.3   Notify HM Coroner and get his consent for the post mortem.

1.4   Conveyance of Body to Mortuary.

      In all cases, this is only done after examination by the Pathologist and on his
      instructions.

      Continuity must be maintained with the transport of the body. The constable
      who arrived at the scene in the first instance should accompany the body to the
      mortuary and be present when identification is made, prior to the Pathologist
      performing the post mortem.

1.5   Arrange for access to the mortuary and, in accordance withany       request from
      the Pathologist, arrange for call-out of mortuary staff.
