PA SYSTEMS IN SPORT STADIA

I It may help the deliberations of the Working Party if I set out
some of the things which should be considered in any follow-up work
to establish criteria for PA systems.

2 Information is needed on the range of crowd and activity noise
levels likely to be encountered in the stadia to which the guidance
applies. This information should include levels, spectral content,
and temporal content.

3 A method is needed to establish the clarity of reproduction of
messages and thus their intelligibility. This does not simply mean
being able to hear them above a background noise but being able to
understand them. In some sitautions messages in a quiet stadium
cannot be understood (eg the infamous BR announcements).

4 In establishing performance criteria consideration will need to be
given to the form of the message and the character of the voice.

5 I do not think that we (NVS) should take on the task of producing
a Standard but it might be appropriate for us to oversee its
production. If HSE wants to take on responsibility for this we
could commission studies of crowd noise and Chair a working group
from the industry (suppliers and perhaps users of PA systems) to
establish the criteria (presumably users would include the police).
The Audio Engineering Society, no doubt, would help in forming such a
Group.
