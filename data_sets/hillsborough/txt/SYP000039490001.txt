             Dear

             I have received a letter from Mr and Mrs Hicks wtiich they have passed on
             to me for attention. It is your letter dated 2 September 1989-

             I have noted the contents of your letter and I am perplexed about the
             suggestion that we are involved in a cover-up of the Bradford City
             football tragedy in 1985. Equally I cannot see what the current
             investigation being carried out by West Yorkshire Police in this Force
             at this time has to do with my personal integrity or that of my officers
             in conducting a thoroughly professional inquiry into the Hillsborough
             tragedy. If you have such evidence to suggest any impropriety I would
             be pleased to hear it. If you have not I would suggest that you keep
             wise counsel.

             I have passed a copy of your letter to the Secretary of the Inquiry.           I
             have also copied this letter to Mr Hicks for his information.

             Yours sincerely




             Assistant Chief Constable
