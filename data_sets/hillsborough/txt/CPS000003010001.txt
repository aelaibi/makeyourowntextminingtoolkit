HILLSBOROUGH

1    I thought it would be helpful to write, please forgive the formality.

2    The terms of reference of the scrutiny being led by Lord Justice Stuart-Smith
     are:

      "To ascertain whether any evidence exists relating to the disaster at the
      Hillsborough Stadium on 15 April 1989 which was not available:

      (a) To the inquiry conducted by the late Lord Taylor; or

      (b) To the Director of Public Prosecutions or the Attorney General for the
      purpose of discharging their respective statutory responsibilities; or

      (c) To the Chief Officer of the South Yorkshire Police in relation to police
      disciplinary matters;

      And in relation to (a) to advise whether any evidence not previously available
      is of such significance as to justify establishment by the Secretary of State for
      the Home Department of a further Public Inquiry; and in relation to (b) and (c)
      to draw to their attention any evidence not previously considered by them which
      may be relevant to their respective duties; and to advise whether there is any
      other action which should be taken in the public interest."

3    These terms are widely drawn in the sense that "any other action ... in the
     public interest" could in theory amount to virtually anything. We will need to
     keep a very close watch on proceedings before the scrutiny to ensure that the
     constitutional position of the Director and the CPS is not compromised in some
     way.

4    Inevitably we will be asked to let the scrutiny know upon precisely what
      material our original (ie. 1990) conclusion of insufficient evidence was based.
      In addition, we will be asked the same question in relation to the material you
      have seen which gave rise to the conclusion in 1997 that there is no material
      which would cause the CPS to alter its earlier decision.

5    To address these points, please will you prepare two schedules of material, one
     for the 1990 work and the other for the 1997 work. You should not do the 1990
     work yourself, but will you please supervise it. I suggest you speak to PJ about
               releasing a caseworker to do the 1990 work, and also to help you do the 1997
               work.

6             Please will you give some thought as to the format of the schedules. First of all,
              we need to identify the types of material we hold (or have seen). The obvious
              ones I am aware of are: statements (whether in CJA form or not), reports (eg.
              the Taylor report), statements under caution (if there are any), interviews (eg.
              under caution) whether taped, contemporaneously recorded or other, transcripts
              of proceedings (eg. of the inquest), photographs, video tapes. You will probably
              want to group together the various types of material for ease of reference in the
              future.

7             I am aware that there are a huge number of photographs taken after the event.
               I do not think it necessary to describe each one individually. It should be
              possible to describe each bundle in a fairly simple way.

8             In relation to some of the types of material, it will be important to identify
               whether we hold the original or a copy. Obviously this is not an issue for such
               items as the Taylor report, but it is crucial so far as video tapes are concerned.
               Where the issue is likely to be critical, we will need to be as detailed as possible
               about the generation of the copy.

9             There may be other issues of importance, such as the date when items have been
              received by us and where items are held if they have been considered by us but
              are not held here.

10            To get the ball rolling, I attach a draft schedule. Please will you consider
              whether this is adequate. It may help if we have a discussion, please will you
              fix this up.

     RE:    THE HILLSBOROUGH SCRUTINY

     Further to the Director’s letter of 30 July 1997,1 enclose two schedules showing the material
